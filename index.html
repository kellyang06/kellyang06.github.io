about

I'm a junior at Fairview High School based in Boulder, CO. I hope to explore and build new projects relating to STEM that I am passionate about!

Previously, I've worked as a high school intern at the University of Colorado Boulder leveraging Maker technologies to create accessible materials for blind or visually impaired clients. Over the course of a month, I designed and built an interactive storytelling setup catered specifically to the needs of a three year old with cortical visual impairment (CVI). This summer, I worked under the City of Boulder's Housing and Human Services department as part of the youth opportunities program. I collaborated on youth-focused projects with local organizations and engaged in event planning, data analysis, and enhancing connections through the Child Friendly Cities Initiative.

Currently, I've been working on a research project on sensors for electronic skin. These sensors could significantly improve tactile sensing ability in prosthetics, and further improve the quality of life for users who use prosthetic devices.

Aside from personal work, I am actively involved in my community, teaching math to elementary school students, hosting monthly talks to educate students about science, planning a day of remembrance for the Table Mesa tragedy, and helping coordinate events for the local library.
